Distinctions

Connecticut Technology Student Association: 3rd Place Coding

The Coding Challenge is a 2-hour long hackathon event where students develop an application that solves the given problem. This year, the challenge was to design an application that encrypts and decrypts given messages using ciphers.

NCWIT's Aspirations in Computing: National Award Winner

The National Center for Women in Computing (NCWIT) sponsors the Aspirations for Computing award, which is given to female high school students that display an aptitude and aspirations in computer science, demonstrated by their computing experience, leadership qualities, community service in STEM, plans for the future, and success despite adversities. Around 40 women are selected every year to receive the National Award.

To learn more about this award, please visit: www.aspirations.org/recognitions/AiCAward

Bruce E. Douglas Scholarship for Overcoming Adversity

This scholarship is given to students that have overcome significant obstacles to achieve a life goal and further use what they have learned throughout their struggles to help others in their community.

To learn more about this award, please visit: http://www.crec.org/foundation/bruce.php
