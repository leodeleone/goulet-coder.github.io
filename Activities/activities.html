Activities

College

Coding && Community

Coding && Community is a STEM-themed community service club that seeks to close the gender and race disparities in the field of computer science. I first discovered Coding && Community as a freshman during the annual Activity Fair. After attending a couple of their general meetings, I applied and accepted a volunteering position as a Java Instructor for Troy High School students. During this time, I attended their high school computer science class and directly mentored students through the programs that they were designing. Not only did this solidify my skills and confidence in Java programming, but it also helped students who were not as fortunate enough to attend a school with a solid curriculum for computer science education. I want to help these students have better access to a STEM education, and therefore, Coding && Community would like to establish an AP Computer Science A course by the end of 2023.

In addition to being a Java Instructor, I also volunteer as a STEP Instructor for Coding && Community's STEP program. The STEP program teaches computer science principles to middle and high school students in the Troy area. One of my main responsibilities is to develop the brand new curriculum for Cryptography and Cybersecurity, and taught the curriculum virtually (due to the COVID-19 pandemic).

If you would like to learn more about Coding && Community, please visit: https://codingandcommunity.org/

Association for Computing Machinery - Women's Division (ACM-W)

The ACM-W division supports female and non-binary students pursuing a degree in Computer Science. Weekly meetings are held in which members can be mentored by Comptuer Science professors. ACM-W also runs a mentor program for first-year students majoring in computer science, in which we assist them in progressing through their freshman year.

If you would like to learn more about ACM-W, please visit: https://women.acm.org/

National Girls Collaborative Project (NGCP) - National Girls Advisory Board

The primary goal of the NGCP is to encourage girls to the fields of STEM. As a member of the NGCP Girls Advisory Board, I provide feedback to the projects sponsored by the NGCP. I offer support for projects through volunteering, and as a Collegiate Member, I mentor the younger women on the Advisory Board!

If you would like to learn more about the National Girls Collaborative Project, please visit: https://ngcproject.org/

High School

The Connecticut Technology Student Association (CT-TSA)

The Technology Student Association is a nation-wide STEM club designed for middle and high school students who are interested in STEM. Throughout the academic year, students design various STEM-related projects and submit them for state and nation-wide competitions.

During my involvement in middle school, I remained an active member of the organization. I explored various projects in STEM, such as Dragster Design, Digital Photography, and Web Development. I placed in numerous state-level competitions, including 3rd place in the Technology Bowl (a jeopardy-like competition where teams of three attempt to answer tech-related questions correctly) and 1st Place in Video Game Design (development done in GameMaker). In the eighth grade, I was also elected to serve as the Vice President for my middle school's division.

I continued to be an active member as a high school student. Throughout my membership, I experimented with multiple aspects of STEM, including Animation Creations and Digital Video Production (placed 2nd), as well as continuing my interests in Software Development and Dragster Design. During the 2018-2019 academic year, I served as the State Treasurer for the Connecticut Technology Student Association. During my one-year term, I represented CT-TSA at the State Capital, managed the funds of CT-TSA, prepare and setup everything needed for the State Conference, and organized a bake sale to support the American Cancer Society, a partner of CT-TSA.

For more information about CT-TSA, please visit: https://www.ct-tsa.net/

For more information about TSA, please visit: https://tsaweb.org/

Girls With Impact Program

The Girls With Impact Program is a 12-week, online course that teaches high school women concepts on how to run a business, demonstrate leadership, and help the community. Throughout the course, I created a business plan for The Hidden Workforce, an online application that will help students with disabilities find jobs.

For more information on the Girls With Impact program, please visit: https://www.girlswithimpact.org/

Bits && Bytes + WiSTEM

Bits && Bytes is a community service club. Computer Science students travel weekly to a local elementary school to teach students how to program in Scratch. This program ends with a tour of the high school STEM classrooms. During my involvement in the club, I helped students create and fix bugs in their Scratch languages, as well as offered a brief introduction to the Java Programming language.

WiSTEM is another community service club. The purpose of WiSTEM is to encourage and support women in STEM. Within the club, we give advice to other community members on seeking out resources and advice that will help meet their needs and help them reach their goals. During the 2019 academic year, WiSTEM partnered with Tallan to offer a school-wide scholarship that would help a female student interested in computer science by supplying them with a laptop and other accessories to help them succeed throughout college.
